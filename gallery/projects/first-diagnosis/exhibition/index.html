First Aid · First Playable

Triage the disaster — then treat each patient.

A passenger jet has crashed in a forest clearing. There are survivors everywhere, equipment scattered in and around the wreckage, fire on three sides, and a clock on every patient. In First Diagnosis, the difference between healing and harm is whether you find the right patient, find the right equipment, and apply it in the right order.

Game Idea

What is First Diagnosis?

A small, browser-based first-aid simulator set in a single disaster site. Every action has a visible consequence — and the wrong order makes the injury worse.

Player goal

Stabilize every survivor before their clock runs out

Walk through a forest clearing with a crashed jet and active fire. Find up to seven patients — some obvious, some hidden. Find the equipment that matches each injury. Examine, judge, act, read the body, adjust.

Core learning shift

Find, then evaluate, then treat

Beginners assume all injuries are treated the same way. In reality, the order matters: a dirty wound must be cleaned before stitches, a deep cut needs more than a surface stitch, and a broken bone won't be helped by medicine alone. The game punishes the wrong sequence — visibly, immediately.

Why this domain

Real first aid is rarely straightforward

Caregivers and doctors adapt on the fly, diagnose with incomplete information, and live with the consequences of a wrong call. That messiness — multiple patients, scattered equipment, a ticking clock — is exactly what makes it playable.

How to play

The five-step loop, scaled to a disaster

The first playable is a single mission: the jet crash. Every patient follows the same loop. The whole mission follows a larger loop around it.

1 · Move

Walk the clearing with WASD or the arrow keys. Trees, the jet, and patches of fire block your path. The world scrolls under your feet.

2 · Find

Some patients are obvious. Others are hidden behind the jet, under debris, or in clusters of trees. Walk close to reveal them, then walk closer to start their 170-second clock.

3 · Search

Saline, small bandages, sutures, and medicine are in the sidebar — always available. Casts, big bandages, cleaning cloths, and water are scattered around the site. You have to pick them up before you can use them.

4 · Treat

Press E (or click) on a found patient to enter treatment mode. The world keeps running. Click the injury, choose the right equipment, watch the action take time.

5 · Adjust

If three patients are lost, the mission ends. Read the body — bleeding, popping stitches, swelling — and try a different plan. Over-treating is just as wrong as under-treating.

Repetition = skill

By the third patient, you'll start predicting which tool matches which injury before the body tells you. That's the learning.
