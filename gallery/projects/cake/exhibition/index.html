Cake

A small baking game about reading the order before you start.

When I was young, I often baked cakes with my mother during birthdays. I enjoyed decorating the cakes and learned that baking requires following steps carefully. This game is built around that experience: read the customer's order, plan, then bake the right cake under pressure.

Play the Game →
Observe

Read the order

Each customer names a base, a frosting, a number of layers, and a set of decorations. Beginners often skip this step. The game makes it the first action.

Judge

Plan under pressure

Higher levels add a timer. You have to decide which ingredient to choose, when to bake, and which decorations matter — before the time runs out.

Act & Adjust

Build, then submit

Mix, bake, decorate, and hand the cake to the customer. Decorations can be removed before submit. The customer reacts with a smile or a frown.

Player Goal

Make a cake that matches every detail of the customer's order, and deliver it before time runs out.

Core Learning Shift

The player learns to shift from making a cake quickly to planning their actions so they can make the correct cake efficiently under pressure.

The game is not about reflexes. It is about reading carefully, planning, and using feedback to do better on the next attempt. The timer is there to make the planning matter.
