Trail Run — Learn to control your pace, manage your fuel, and find the right path

▶ Start Running

About

You are a trail runner making your way up a mountain. You need to control your speed, choose the correct path, and refuel on time.

Core lesson: The most common beginner mistake is sprinting from the start — real experts know when to push, when to ease off, when to eat a gel, and when to drink water.

Domain Knowledge

Real trail running challenges

Trail running is nothing like road running: surfaces change between dirt, stone steps, and concrete; gradients shift between uphill, downhill, and flat. Energy drains much faster — you must learn to manage fuel and hydration.

Common beginner mistakes

Beginners often think "faster is better" — they exhaust themselves early and crash in the second half.

The shift

The runner learns to pace themselves, pick the right forks, and time their intake of energy and water.

System Design

Environment

Surface

  • Dirt — medium friction, high trip risk
  • Stone — good grip, medium trip risk
  • Concrete — low friction (slippery), low trip risk

Gradient

  • Uphill — speed -60%, drain x1.8
  • Flat — normal speed, normal drain
  • Downhill — speed +40%, drain x1.3, high trip risk

Player Stats

Core Values

  • Sugar — drains while running, refuel with gel
  • Electrolytes — drains with sweat, refuel with salt pills
  • Water — drains with sweat, refuel by drinking
  • Health — drops from injury/low energy/dehydration
  • Distance — only increases on the correct path
  • Climb — total elevation gained

Controls

  • [Up/W] Accelerate / [Down/S] Decelerate
  • [<-/A] [->/D] Steer left/right
  • [1] Salt pill +electrolytes
  • [2] Energy gel +sugar
  • [3] Drink water +hydration
  • [4] Bandage wound +health
  • [M] Toggle GPS map

Feedback

• Low energy (sugar/electrolytes) + low speed — prompts to eat/drink, speed cap reduced

• Low water (dehydrated) — speed cap reduced, continuous health drain

• Leg injury — wound marker on screen, press [4] to bandage

• Distance stalled — wrong fork, press [M] to check GPS

Win/Lose

Win: Reach the finish line after completing all segments.

Lose: Health drops to 0 (injury, exhaustion, dehydration, or falling off a cliff).
