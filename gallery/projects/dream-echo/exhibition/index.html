dream

A web game about paying attention to what someone truly needs — not what you think they need.

"What you think about in the day, you will dream of at night." But what you think is good for someone might not be what they actually need.

What is this game?

In dream, you guide a character through their daytime activities, trying to help them have a good dream that night. The catch: doing more "good things" is not always the answer. Some activities look helpful but don't match the character's real needs. Others build up too much excitement, so even a "good" dream becomes exhausting. You have to read the character — their words, their reactions — and choose activities that truly fit them.

Player Goal

Help the character wake up energized after a good dream — not tired from a nightmare, and not drained from a dream that was too exciting.

Core Learning Shift

🤔
Novice Mindset
Just do lots of "good" things during the day. More kindness, more fun, more activities = better dream.
🎯
Expert Mindset
Pay attention to the character's real difficulties and preferences. Choose carefully — the right few activities, not every activity. Restraint and attunement matter more than quantity.

The Hidden Trap

Even a dream that is too exciting can leave you tired when you wake up. More isn't always better — the sweet spot is what counts.
