Shark Bite

A diving game where you explore a living coral reef, discover creatures hiding in the coral, and maintain the health of the ecosystem. The ocean is something to understand, not fear — and a healthy reef is full of life most people never see.

Play the game Read the design

Game idea

A short concept you can read in 30 seconds.

Concept

You are a diver exploring a coral reef. Swim near coral formations to discover creatures hiding inside — clownfish in anemones, eels in crevices, parrotfish grazing. Clean pollution to keep the reef healthy while managing oxygen, depth, and equipment.

Player goal

Discover enough species to fill your catalog AND keep the reef's eco-health above the target. Read coral glow for hidden creatures, clean drifting pollution, and learn that a big blip on radar is rarely a dangerous one.

Core learning shift

The shift this game is built around

People see the ocean as something to fear — especially sharks — because the only information that tends to go around is how dangerous it is. In reality, the ocean is beautiful, and knowing true information lets people see that.

This is not a theme painted on a generic game. The coral-hiding discovery mechanic, the pollution-cleaning loop, the radar that beeps for size instead of danger, and the penalty for startling harmless creatures all exist to make this shift playable.
