Domain Knowledge & System Design

How real-world cave exploration knowledge becomes a playable game system.

Domain: Cave Exploration

Real-World Domain

Cave exploration combines adventure and problem-solving skills in an unusual environment. Explorers explore nature and navigate unknown places while getting used to the unexpected obstacles they may encounter.

Novice Misconception

Beginners often underestimate the importance of navigation and managing resources. They may think that every random tunnel they find will lead to success and collect items that are not useful to complete the task. Beginners make the mistake of rushing forward without carefully observing their surroundings — they choose the closest path, ignore warning signs, or use their resources too early without thinking about future challenges.

Expert Judgment

The biggest challenge is making decisions in an unpredictable and strange environment while taking different factors into consideration. The player must account for the energy required to perform certain tasks and manage their depleted resources (flashlight, harnesses to climb inside the cave). Experts observe carefully, read environmental clues, and conserve resources for when they are truly needed.

Core Learning Shift

Moving from exploring by instinct to making careful decisions and resource management. This shift becomes playable through the game's observe-judge-act loop, where rushing forward leads to failure and careful observation leads to success.

Feedback Translation

Invisible game data is translated into readable feedback for the player:

🔦

Flashlight Flickering

Invisible data: Battery is low. Visible feedback: The flashlight light becomes dimmer and flickers randomly. Battery bar color shifts from green → yellow → red.

💥

Loud Rumbling + Screen Shake

Invisible data: Cave may collapse soon. Visible feedback: The screen shakes and dust particles fall from the ceiling while a low rumbling sound plays.

👣

Footprints on the Floor

Invisible data: The player may be close to a safe route or the exit. Visible feedback: Footprint marks appear on the floor, hinting at a direction to explore.

🦇

Bat Sounds & Dark Shapes

Invisible data: A bat colony is nearby. Visible feedback: Fluttering dark shapes and high-pitched sounds warn of the hazard before contact.

🩸

Red Vignette Overlay

Invisible data: Health, battery, or time is critically low (danger level 2). Visible feedback: A pulsing red vignette appears around the screen edges.

Success & Failure Conditions

✓ Success Condition

The player succeeds if they reach the exit of the cave within the time limit while still being able to get through obstacles and rescuing the missing explorer.

✗ Failure Conditions

  • Out of time: The timer reaches zero before escaping.
  • Out of light: Flashlight battery reaches zero and the player gets lost in darkness.
  • Out of health: Health reaches zero from hazards (bats, water, traps, collapse).
  • Drowning: Stepping into underground water without enough health.
  • Hidden traps: Falling for a trap that deals lethal damage.
  • Cave collapse: Getting caught in a collapse.

Just-Right Ranges & Thresholds

Moving too quickly increases the risk of triggering hazards, but moving too slowly wastes time. Using the flashlight too much wastes energy, but using too little makes navigation dangerous. The game rewards balanced, deliberate play.

Challenge Presets

Each level changes system variables and relationships — not just visual decoration:

Preset 1: The Entrance Level 1

What changes: Generous time (120s) and battery (80%) give room to learn. Only one hazard type (bats). Footprints clearly mark the safe path. No rescue objective — just escape.

Variables: time=120, battery=80, health=100, hazards=[bats], rescueRequired=false
Relationships: battery_drain=normal, hazard_damage=low, clue_visibility=high

Preset 2: The Depths Level 2

What changes: More time (150s) but less battery (70%) and a larger map. Two new hazards (water, traps) with higher damage. Rescue objective introduced — must find explorer before escaping. Footprints guide but dead ends exist.

Variables: time=150, battery=70, health=100, hazards=[water, traps], rescueRequired=true
Relationships: battery_drain=normal, hazard_damage=high, clue_visibility=medium, map_complexity=high

Preset 3: The Escape Level 3

What changes: Tightest time (100s) and lowest battery (60%). Unstable ceiling introduces periodic collapse warnings (screen shake + dust). All hazard types active. Must rescue and escape under maximum pressure.

Variables: time=100, battery=60, health=100, hazards=[bats, water, unstable], rescueRequired=true
Relationships: battery_drain=normal, hazard_damage=high, collapse_events=periodic, clue_visibility=medium
