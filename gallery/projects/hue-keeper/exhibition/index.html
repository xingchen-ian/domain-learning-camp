Hue Keeper

A color theory game where you restore an ancient fresco by collecting, mixing, and matching colors — while defending against monsters that can only be defeated by their complementary opposite.

Play the Game Learn the Domain

Game Idea

What is Hue Keeper, and what does the player learn?

🎨

Concept

You play as Pixie, a tiny guardian of an ancient cave fresco. The fresco's colors are fading. Venture into the garden, collect colors from flowers, mix them at the fountain, and restore the fresco before it fades away. In later levels, monsters emerge — and only complementary colors can defeat them.

🎯

Player Goal

Fill every broken area of the fresco with the correct color. In Level 3, also survive monster attacks by splattering them with their complementary color — the opposite on the color wheel. Using the same color as a monster makes it stronger!

💡

Core Learning Shift

Players begin thinking "match the same color." Through gameplay, they shift to a comprehensive technique of judging, mixing, and matching — understanding complementary colors, efficiency under time pressure, and strategic color choice.

Three Levels of Challenge

Each level introduces a new color theory concept.

Level 1

Color Matching

Collect primary colors (Red, Blue, Yellow) from flowers and match them to the fresco's missing areas. No time pressure — learn the basics of observation and matching.

Level 2

Color Mixing

The fresco needs five secondary colors (Green, Orange, Purple, and repeats). Only primary-color flowers grow in the garden. Mix two primaries at the fountain — each splattered color fades over 60 seconds, so work fast!

Level 3

Color Conflict

Six fresco pieces need restoring while monsters emerge and chase Pixie. Each monster has a color — only the complementary color defeats it. The same color makes it stronger. Colors fade 60 seconds after splattering.
