Domain Knowledge & System Design

Why sailing is hard, why the no-go zone exists, and how the game turns it into a learnable loop.

1. The real-world domain

The designer joined a beginner sailing lesson and discovered an idea that felt almost unfair: the boat does not go where you point it. Speed depends on the angle between the wind and the boat, the angle of the sail, and the boat’s current speed.

Real sailboats move in the direction of the resultant of three vectors: the wind force on the sail, the water resistance on the hull, and the rudder. Adjusting any one of them changes the others.

2. The novice misconception

Beginners assume: point the boat at the target, go.

This fails for three reasons:

  1. No-go zone. If the wind is blowing roughly toward the bow, the boat stalls. It physically cannot move forward.
  2. Sail trim. The sail must be at a working angle to the wind. A sail aligned with the wind is useless.
  3. Route, not heading. To go upwind, the boat must zigzag — sail to one side of the wind, turn through it, sail to the other side. The route is what reaches the target, not the heading.

3. The core learning shift

Beginner: sailing = steering toward the target.
Expert: sailing = reading the wind, then choosing boat angle, sail angle, and route in relation to it.

The game is built so the player can feel this shift through repetition, not through reading. They point the boat at the target, nothing happens, they read the wind, they tack, they reach the target.

5. Data model

Environment (driven by the level)

  • Wind direction (radians)
  • Wind strength (0–1)
  • Water current direction & speed
  • Rock positions
  • Target position
  • Game time / time limit
  • Boat health (starts at 100)

Player-controlled

  • Boat direction — A / D
  • Sail angle — W / S
  • Path choice — implicit, by holding a heading over time
  • Turn timing — implicit, by releasing A / D

System-calculated results

  • Boat speed (px / s)
  • Sideways drift (combined into speed for the v1)
  • Stability (visual heel)
  • Distance to target
  • Time remaining
  • Collision risk (rocks)

7. Challenge presets

Each level changes the system, not just the visuals.

LevelWindCurrentRocksTime limitWhat it teaches
1 — Crosswind Crosswind (blows across the path) None 0 None Boat does not go where you point. You need to angle across the wind.
2 — Headwind Headwind (blows from target toward you) None 0 70 s You cannot sail straight at the target. You must tack.
3 — Rock field Crosswind None 6 110 s Route planning under time pressure with obstacles.

8. Win and lose

Win: boat health > 0 and game time > 0 when the boat reaches the target.

Lose: boat health ≤ 0 or game time ≤ 0 before reaching the target.
