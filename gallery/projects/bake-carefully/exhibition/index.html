Bake Carefully

A baking game about patience, precision, and learning from your mistakes

▶ Play the Game

Game Idea

Bake four different goods — cookies, bread, brownies, and cake — by selecting the right ingredients, mixing at the right time, and pulling them out of the oven before they burn.

🎯

Player Goal

Successfully bake all 4 items. Each recipe tests a different skill: ingredient knowledge, mixing timing, and oven monitoring.

🧠

Core Learning Shift

Beginners think baking is just following a recipe. Experts know you must check materials first, understand your equipment, and bake carefully with full attention.

⏱️

Main Challenge

Time is everything. Leave food in the oven too long and it burns. Don't pay attention, and you lose. Watch, judge, and act at the right moment.

🔄

Learning Loop

Observe → Judge → Act → Read Feedback → Adjust. Every failure teaches you what to do differently next time.

Domain Knowledge

The real-world domain is baking baked goods — something the student loves but learned the hard way is harder than it looks.

The Real Experience

Baking is a craft where things don't always go your way. Ingredients can be wrong, machines can malfunction, and timing is critical. The student learned through experience that success comes from preparation, attention, and understanding your tools — not just reading a recipe.

Novice Misconception

❌ What Beginners Think

"You just need to follow a recipe for it to work." Beginners grab ingredients, throw them together, put everything in the oven, and expect perfect results. They don't check if ingredients are correct, don't test their equipment, and don't pay close attention to timing.

Expert Judgment

✅ What Experts Do

Experts learn how to use their equipment first. They check their materials before starting. They understand that different recipes need different leavening agents (baking soda vs. baking powder vs. yeast), different mixing times, and different bake times. They watch the oven and use visual and auditory cues — color change, the smell of doneness, the "ding" of readiness, and the warning sign of smoke.

Why This Becomes Playable

The gap between novice and expert thinking maps directly to game mechanics:

Real-World Skill Game Mechanic What the Player Learns
Checking ingredients before starting Pantry phase: select correct ingredients from similar-looking options Baking soda ≠ baking powder ≠ yeast — each does something different
Understanding your equipment Mixing phase: stop the mixer at the right time Under-mixing = lumpy dough, over-mixing = tough dough
Paying attention to timing Baking phase: watch color change, listen for the ding, remove before smoke Food goes from raw → golden → burnt in seconds. Watch carefully.
Learning from mistakes Result screen with specific feedback, then retry Each failure teaches what to adjust on the next attempt

System Design

The game system is built around four data categories: environment data, player-controlled data, system-calculated results, and feedback translation.

System Graph

Data Model

🌐 Environment Data

  • Recipe definitions (ingredients, timing, temperature)
  • Oven state (on/off, temperature, timer)
  • Food readiness state (raw → golden → burnt)
  • Pantry inventory (correct + wrong items)

🎮 Player-Controlled Data

  • Selected ingredients from pantry
  • Mixer start and stop actions
  • Oven put-in and remove actions
  • Recipe selection from menu

📊 Calculated Results

  • Ingredient accuracy (correct vs. wrong picks)
  • Mix quality (under / good / over)
  • Bake quality (under / good / burnt)
  • Total score (0–100) and pass/fail

💬 Feedback Translation

  • Smoke = food is burning (visual)
  • Ding sound = food is ready (auditory)
  • Color change = bake progress (visual)
  • ✓/✗ marks = ingredient correctness (state)

Rules, Boundaries & Outcomes

📋 Important States

Food visibly changes appearance based on bake time — from pale/raw to golden brown to dark/burnt. Ingredient correctness is shown with ✓ (correct) or ✗ (wrong) marks. The oven timer bar shows progress with a green "ideal zone" and a red "burn zone."

🏆 Success Condition

Finish baking all 4 items (Cookies, Bread, Brownies, Cake) with a total score of 60 or higher. Score is weighted: Ingredients 35%, Mixing 25%, Baking 40%.

💥 Failure Conditions

Burning the food (leaving it in the oven past the burn threshold), using wrong ingredients (e.g., baking soda instead of baking powder for cake), or using wrong technique (under-mixing or over-mixing the dough).

Challenge Presets

Each recipe is a challenge preset that changes real variables — not just visual decoration. Different ingredients, different timing windows, and different difficulty levels.

Recipe Difficulty Key Ingredients Wrong Substitutes Mix Target Bake Ideal What Changes
🍪 Cookies Easy Flour, Sugar, Butter, Egg, Choc Chips, Baking Soda Baking Powder, Salt, Yeast 2.5s (±1.2) 7s (±2.5) Fewer ingredients, wide timing windows
🍞 Bread Medium Flour, Water, Yeast, Salt, Oil Baking Soda, Baking Powder, Sugar 3.5s (±1.0) 10s (±2.0) Requires yeast (not soda), longer mix & bake
🟫 Brownies Medium Flour, Sugar, Cocoa, Butter, Egg, Choc Chips Baking Soda, Baking Powder, Yeast 2.0s (±0.8) 8s (±1.5) No leavening needed, tighter bake window
🎂 Cake Hard Flour, Sugar, Butter, Egg, Milk, Baking Powder, Vanilla Baking Soda, Yeast, Cocoa 3.0s (±0.7) 12s (±1.5) Most ingredients, tightest windows, uses baking powder

🔑 The Key Learning Difference

Cookies use Baking Soda. Bread uses Yeast. Brownies use no leavening. Cake uses Baking Powder. A beginner grabs whatever looks similar. An expert knows the difference — and that's the core learning shift this game teaches.
