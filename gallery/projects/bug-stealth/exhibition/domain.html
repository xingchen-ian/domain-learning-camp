Domain Knowledge

Domain: plant care

Core experience: tending a flycatcher

The real-world domain

The student (陈晴晴) once kept a flycatcher (pitcher plant). It died. From that experience she learned that growing a carnivorous plant depends on three things — sunlight, soil / fertilizer and a clean water source — and that the flycatcher itself preys on insects. Insects are therefore both a real gardening threat and a natural part of the plant's micro-ecology.

Novice misconception

Beginners think the only job is to avoid the insects, and they ignore everything else — they forget to water, forget to feed, never adjust the light. The plant quietly dies while the player is busy running away.

Expert judgement

An experienced plant keeper pays attention to all of the details at once: the angle of the mirrors that redirect sunlight, the timing and position of insect attacks, and the locations of the water bottle and the fertilizer. Avoiding insects is necessary but not sufficient — the plant still has to be fed.

Why this becomes playable

The constant threat of the insects provides the stress that forces the player to notice the smaller details (supply locations, light path, timing). That tension is exactly the learning shift: beginners will notice the details after the stress of the insect attack. The game makes the invisible variables of plant care — water, fertilizer, sunlight — into visible objects you must physically carry and place while under attack.

Core player loop

StageWhat the player does
ObserveMirror angles, insect attacks, fertilizer and water-bottle sites.
JudgeTiming of the insect attack, the path of the light rays, the positioning of other elements.
ActWalk, crouch to take cover, climb plants, grab objects, carry & reposition mirrors.
Read feedbackThe plant grows slightly taller when you water it, fertilize it, or expose it to sunlight.
AdjustReposition mirrors, collect supplies or hide in shadows based on real-time feedback.
