Win / Lose Conditions

Win

  • Time remaining > 0
  • Reach the finish flag
  • No bars dropped (or accept dropped bars in early levels)
  • Horse still willing to keep going

Lose

  • Time runs out
  • Too many bars dropped
  • Horse becomes too angry and refuses to jump
  • Player gives up

Player Input

Input Mapping Riding meaning
W / ↑ Accelerate Ask the horse to move up a gear
S / ↓ Decelerate Ask the horse to slow down
A / ← Steer left Reins left - the horse turns left (only if she is willing)
D / → Steer right Reins right - the horse turns right (only if she is willing)
Space Jump Ask for a takeoff - timing window matters
E Soothe Voice + hand, calm the horse, raise its mood
F Whip Force the horse to go faster - lowers mood
P Pause Take a breath
R Restart Reset the current course
V Cycle camera view Behind → Top-down → Side. Behind view aligns the horse head-on to each fence (realistic approach); Side shows stride and arc; Top-down reads the line.

Game Environment Variables

Variable What it represents in real riding
Obstacle positionWhere the jump is on the course
Route lengthTotal distance to the finish
Time limitCourse time limit for that level
Target positionThe finish flag
Obstacle shapeVertical, oxer, wall - each needs a different line
Obstacle height scaleReal show-jumping scale: top rail at roughly the horse's withers (0.85–1.10× horse scale); wall stands a little taller than a rail
Obstacle spacingGenerous gaps (~85–115 world units between fences) so the rider can reset speed and line before each jump
Different horsesWhisper, Dapple, Storm - three temperaments
Horse emotionsHappy / calm / tired / angry - live system
Obedience factor0–1: how much the horse actually listens to your reins (derived from emotion)

Challenge Presets

Level 1 - First Canter

TargetPass all obstacles and reach the destination
ChallengeBuild workable judgments for speed, line and take-off point
RouteStraight line across open meadow
Fences8 verticals / oxers
TimeNo time limit
HorseWhisper - gentle, willing, slow to anger

Level 2 - Winding Path

TargetPass all obstacles within a time limit
ChallengeAdjust your judgments as the situation changes; manage mood on a longer course
RouteS-curve across wide grassland with mixed obstacle types
Fences10 mixed (verticals, oxers, walls)
Time90-second limit
HorseDapple - gentle, but the route and clock will test you

Level 3 - Trust the Bond

TargetPass all 14 fences within tight time without dropping bars
ChallengeBuild trust: Storm will ignore your reins when angry, refuse jumps, and bolt. Soothe her or fail.
RouteLong roundabout across grassland, many turns
Fences14 mixed (verticals, oxers, walls)
Time80-second limit
HorseStorm - strong but bad-tempered; high angerRate means she gets upset fast

Feedback Translation

What the player does wrong What the system shows Why it matters
Speed too slow or too fast Bar falls, jump is missed, horse loses balance Speed has a sweet spot for each obstacle
Wrong line / wrong take-off point Bar falls, horse has to refuse A jump needs a workable approach
Whipping a tired horse Horse mood drops, horse eventually refuses Force breaks the relationship
Ignoring the horse's mood Horse stops listening, course fails A horse is a partner, not a tool
Horse angry: you steer but she drifts her own way "!! YOUR HORSE IS NOT LISTENING !!" warning banner; steering input ignored or reduced to ~20% A frightened horse pulls against your hands. Soothe (E) before she bolts.
Horse angry: you ask to jump but she refuses "Won't jump!" feedback; obstacle remains un-cleared Below emotion 40, refusal probability rises sharply. Keep her above 50.
Horse very angry (emotion <12): she bolts "Bolting!" - horse runs off on her own at full speed, ignoring your throttle The most dangerous state. Only soothe can bring her back.
Good plan and good ride Clean jump, "Beautiful!" feedback, mood + Reward for correct judgment and respect

The Core Learning Loop

Observe the speed, the destination, the route, the take-off point, the obstacle, the horse's mood.
Judge whether the speed is right, whether the line is workable, whether the take-off point is in the window, whether the horse is willing.
Act: adjust the speed, steer the line, ask for a jump, soothe when needed, whip only as a last resort.
Read feedback: did the horse clear it, did the bar fall, what is the horse's mood now?
Adjust for the next obstacle and the next run.
