Game Idea

You are trapped inside a research lab and must reach the exit without being killed. Enemies patrol the rooms, doors can block sight, and glass walls let you see through but cannot be walked through. The game is inspired by Hotline Miami: quick, readable action where how you move matters as much as how you fight.

Player goal: reach the green EXIT door of each level alive.
Core learning shift: beginners realise that different weapon choices allow for different gameplay — a knife keeps you silent, a shotgun clears rooms fast but alerts everyone, and a silenced pistol sits in between.

Why this domain matters

New players often treat shooters as pure aiming challenges. The domain challenge here is understanding enemy movement and perception. Every guard or dog has a vision cone, speed, and alert state. The player must observe these, decide whether to sneak past, open a door, or fight, and then read the feedback (detection bar, health, red vignette) to improve the next attempt.

The novice misconception is that the only thing that matters is clicking on enemies. Through repetition, players learn that the environment and weapon choice are tools just as important as aim.
