Controls

No install, no internet—just open and play. Inside a level, press Space to see the controls and begin; press Space any time to pause / resume (the timer freezes with you).

W / S — throttle forward / brake & reverse
A — strafe left　D — strafe right
U / H — ascend / descend
Space — pause / resume (shows this guide)
When you let go, the drone doesn't stop instantly—it keeps gliding on its momentum, and the wind keeps nudging it off course. To hold a straight line you have to anticipate the drift and steer against it early.

Domain Knowledge

The Real Domain

The creator loves flying drones but finds plain sightseeing boring. The real fun is navigation—threading through an obstacle-filled forest, with the wind as an invisible enemy.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Assuming the drone flies straight wherever the nose points.
  • Not realizing the drone keeps drifting forward on momentum after you release the controls.
  • Ignoring how the wind keeps changing your actual heading.
  • Only letting go when a crash is already unavoidable—too late.

What Experts Do

Beginners hold throttle and hope for a straight line; experts anticipate, make small corrections early, and counter the wind's push with opposite inputs to keep their path straight.

Why It Works as a Game

The game makes the invisible visible: the WIND readout shows speed and direction, momentum lets you feel the drone keep gliding after you let go, and the enlarged ALTITUDE gauge plus distance-to-LAND bar give instant feedback. Through repeated failures and adjustments, players shift their thinking from novice to expert.

System Design

The game is driven by three clearly separated groups of data:

Environment Data (the world)

  • Wind—speed + direction, constantly pushing the drone off course.
  • Tall trees—fly over them (some tower above the 70 m start height).
  • Overhead cables—dip below the marked height to slip under.
  • LAND pad—the landing target at the end of the course.

Data From Player Controls

  • W/S forward / reverse thrust.
  • A/D strafe left / right.
  • U/H ascend / descend.

Results the System Computes

  • Drone position, velocity, altitude.
  • Whether it hit a tree / cable / touched ground off the pad.
  • Whether it reached the pad and dropped to 0 m.
  • Whether the countdown has run out.

Feedback Translation

  • WIND indicator—arrow shows direction, number shows speed.
  • ALTITUDE gauge—large readout + vertical bar + pad marker line.
  • ⏱ Timer—turns red and flashes when 10 seconds remain.

Win / Lose

  • Win: Fly above the LAND pad and hold H to bring altitude down to 0 m for a smooth touchdown.
  • Lose: Hit a tree, hit a cable, touch ground off the pad, or let the countdown hit zero.

Three Difficulty Tiers (different variables, not just looks)

P1 · Beginner

Calm Glade

  • Short trees, light breeze
  • No cable obstacles
  • Shortest course
⏱ Time limit 60 s
P2 · Intermediate

Crosswind Pass

  • Tall trees (some above start height)
  • Moderate wind · 3 cables
  • Alternate tree / cable gates
⏱ Time limit 40 s
P3 · Challenge

Gust Maze

  • Tallest trees · strong wind
  • 4 cables · longest course
  • Climb over trees / dive under cables
⏱ Time limit 30 s

The harder the tier, the shorter the time limit (60 / 40 / 30 s)—you must fly right and fly fast.

About This 3D Version

  • Rendered with Three.js (r128) in true 3D: chase camera, 3D drone / trees / cables / pad, and floating labels.
  • Three.js is bundled locally (vendor/), so it runs fully offline with zero network calls.
  • Independent from the 2D version: this 3D build runs on its own port and never touches the original 2D game.
  • Levels use a deterministic 'gate scheduler' (tree gates and cable gates at fixed spacing) that guarantees every level is completable.
