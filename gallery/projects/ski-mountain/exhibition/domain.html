Domain Knowledge

The Real Experience

When you ski on a mountain, you realize that you don't simply go the direction you want to go. It depends on the angles of your legs, how much power you put on one leg, your speed, your body angle, when you're turning, and where you're looking at.

Novice Misconception

Beginners think the skis should move directly toward the bottom and not slow down. They point straight downhill, go too fast, and lose control.

Expert Judgment

Experts know they need to plan where they're going and when to turn. The most important domain challenges are:

  • Speed control — S-turns naturally slow you down
  • Route planning — choose paths that avoid obstacles
  • Stay calm — panic leads to bad decisions
  • Know your level — don't attempt terrain beyond your ability

Why This Becomes Playable

The tension between "go fast" and "control speed" is a perfect game mechanic. The screen shakes when you go too fast — that's the teachable moment. Rocks and trees force you to plan routes. Wind and fog add uncertainty. Every run teaches: carving S-turns is the skill that keeps you alive.

Feedback That Teaches

  • Screen shake — you're going too fast straight down. Carve!
  • Red flash — you hit something. Plan a better route!
  • Fog overlay — weather reduces visibility. Slow down!
  • Stop warning — you've stopped. Keep moving!
