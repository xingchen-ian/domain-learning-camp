Domain knowledge & system design

This page is the bridge between "real golf" and "the game." It shows what I learned from playing the sport and how each idea became a variable, a UI element, or a level rule in the system.

The real-world domain

Golf looks physical — you swing a club at a ball — but most of the strokes I lose on the course are lost before I swing. I misread the distance, I grab the wrong club, I aim at a flag without thinking about the bunker in front of it. Real golf is a decision sport dressed up as a power sport.

Novice misconception

"If I just hit it harder / straighter, the ball will go where I want." Beginners over-rely on muscle and under-rely on judgment.

Most important domain challenge

Choosing the right energy for the situation, and choosing the right club for the distance. Beginners pick the club they like, not the club the shot needs.

Core learning shift

From "golf is about hitting the ball" to "golf is about reading distance, picking a club, and managing risk." That shift is what this game tries to produce.

How the domain became a system

Environment data (read each turn)

  • Distance to flag (yards)
  • Traps on the course (water, sand)
  • Trees blocking the line
  • Current stroke count vs. par

Player-controlled data (chosen each swing)

  • Club (driver / 3-wood / 5-iron / 7-iron / wedge)
  • Aim direction (left / right of the line to the flag)
  • Swing power (0–100%)

System-calculated results

  • Where the ball actually lands
  • New distance to flag
  • Whether the ball is on the fairway, in a trap, or OOB

Feedback translation

  • Trap hit → +1 stroke, ball resets to last good position
  • Out of bounds → +1 stroke, ball resets to last good position
  • Short / over → "Aim farther / closer next time"
  • Left / right → "Adjust your aim"

Challenge presets

Each hole changes the system variables, not the visuals alone.

  • Level 1 — Par 3, 100 yds, no traps. Gentle intro: just learn the controls and the idea of distance.
  • Level 2 — Par 4, 200 yds, 3 traps. Now you have to plan a lay-up and a club change.
  • Level 3 — Par 5, 330 yds, 5 traps. Two or three good shots or you will not make par+5.

Win & game over

Hole complete in ≤ par + 5 strokes = win and advance. Exceed par + 5 = game over on that hole.
