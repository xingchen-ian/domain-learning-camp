Golf — a small strategy game about choosing well

I have played golf for two years, and what surprised me most is that it is not really a physical sport. The hard part is deciding: how far is the flag, which club fits the lie, how much power is enough, and where on the fairway should I aim. This game is my attempt to put that decision-making under the player's hands.

Game idea

A top-down, 5-club golf game across three holes

Each hole starts on a tee box, the flag sits somewhere down the fairway, and the player picks an aim direction, a club, and a power level before every swing. The ball lands, gives feedback, and the next decision begins. The goal is to finish the hole in fewer strokes than par + 5. Going over that means game over.

What the player learns

Beginners walk in thinking "if I swing harder, the ball will go where I want." The game gently breaks that assumption. Power without the right club overshoots. A wedge on a short shot underhits. A driver in a tight gap lands in the bunker. After three holes the player should instinctively think: distance first, club second, power last.

How to play

Open Play, pick a hole from the menu, and use:

  • A / D — change aim direction
  • W / S — raise / lower power
  • Space — take the swing
  • Click a club button — switch club
