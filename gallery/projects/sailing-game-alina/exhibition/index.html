Game Idea

Concept: A top-down 2D sailing game where the player navigates a small boat to a target buoy across wind, currents, and rocks. The boat cannot sail directly into the wind — the player must learn to read wind direction, trim the sail to the correct angle, and plan a route (including tacking) to reach the target before time runs out.

Player Goal: Reach the target buoy before time expires, while maintaining boat stability and avoiding rocks.

Novice Thinking
"Sailing means steering toward the target."
→
Expert Thinking
"Sailing means reading the wind and adjusting the boat, sail, and route in relation to it."

Core Learning Shift: The game teaches that you cannot simply point your boat at the destination. The wind direction creates a "no-go zone" — an area about 45 degrees on either side of the wind source where the sail cannot catch wind. To reach a target that lies upwind, the player must tack: sail at an angle to the wind in one direction, then turn and sail at an angle in the other direction, zig-zagging toward the goal. This is the single most important skill the game aims to teach.

Domain Knowledge: Sailing

The Real-World Domain

Sailing is the art of using wind to propel a boat across water. A sailboat has three key controls: the rudder (which direction the boat points), the sail trim (the angle of the sail relative to the boat), and the route (the path chosen to reach the destination). The wind is the engine — but it only provides power when the sail is set at the right angle relative to where the wind is coming from.

Novice Misconception: Beginners believe that to reach a destination, you simply point the boat at it. They don't realize that wind direction creates zones where the boat cannot sail, and that the sail must be continuously adjusted as the wind angle changes.
Expert Judgment: An experienced sailor first reads the wind direction, then chooses a point of sail (the boat's angle relative to the wind). They adjust the sail to the optimal angle for that point of sail, and plan a route that works with the wind rather than against it. When the target is upwind, they tack back and forth — accepting a longer path to maintain speed, rather than fighting the wind and going nowhere.

Why This Knowledge Becomes Playable

Time pressure forces the player to find the most efficient route. The wind direction is visible at all times. The sail angle is directly controlled. Speed responds immediately to trim changes. This means the player can feel the relationship between wind angle, sail trim, and boat speed — and learn through experimentation that tacking is not optional, it is the only way upwind.

Environment Data (set per challenge)

VariableDescription

Wind strengthHow hard the wind blows (affects maximum speed)
Water currentA constant drift applied to the boat's position (shown by green arrow)
Rock positionsObstacles that damage stability on collision
Target positionThe buoy the player must reach
Game timeCountdown timer — reaching zero means failure

Player-Controlled Data

VariableControl
Boat headingLeft / Right arrow keys — steer the boat
Sail angleUp / Down arrow keys — trim sail in or out

StabilityDecreases on rock collisions or high-speed poor trim

Time remainingCountdown from challenge time limit
Collision riskProximity check against all rocks

Feedback Translation

The game translates invisible system data into readable feedback:

🌓
Sail flaps (wavy line)
Sail angle is inefficient or boat is in the no-go zone — adjust your heading or trim
≈
Wake becomes shorter
Speed is dropping — your angle to wind or sail trim has worsened
↑
Wind arrow changes
The target may not be directly reachable — consider a different route
⚠
Red warning rings on rocks
Collision risk — steer away immediately
📊
HUD bars (speed, trim, stability)
Immediate numerical feedback on boat state
💬
"Good trim!" / "Sail is flapping!" messages
Direct text feedback guides the learning loop

Success & Failure Conditions

Success: Reach the target buoy before time expires. Achieved by reading the wind, setting an efficient sail angle, and choosing a route that works with the wind direction.
Failure: Run out of time, or lose all stability (from rock collisions or sustained poor trim at speed).

Challenge Presets

Each challenge changes system variables and relationships — not just visual decoration:

★☆☆

Downwind Run

Weaker wind pushes from behind. A cross-current drifts you east, and four rocks block your path. Teaches basic sail trim while steering around obstacles. 35 seconds.

★★☆

Beam Reach

Target lies perpendicular to the wind. A strong current pushes you south and west — away from the target. Five rocks block the optimal path. 38 seconds.

★★★

Upwind Challenge

Target is directly upwind. Six rocks create a narrow slalom channel. A current pushes you back toward the wind source, fighting your progress. Requires tacking — the core learning shift. 60 seconds.
