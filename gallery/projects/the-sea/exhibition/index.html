The Sea

A first-person diving game about reading water, planning routes, and surviving underwater.

Game Idea

Player goal: reach the target zone with HP > 0, oxygen > 0, and at least 3 species collected, all before the oxygen runs out.

Core learning shift: beginners start by learning to control direction and depth. Through play, they learn to read water currents, avoid dangerous species, drop a surface signal so boats can see them, and pick the right depth to conserve oxygen.

BCD inflation: a critical real-world diving skill. Press I at the surface to inflate your BCD (buoyancy compensator device) — this lets you stay on the surface and slowly regenerate oxygen. Without inflation, you automatically sink back down. Warning: inflating underwater causes fatal barotrauma (HP = 0). You must also deploy a surface signal (O) before inflating — if you stay on the surface with an inflated BCD but no signal, a boat will strike you after 2 seconds.

Real experience source: the design is based on a real diving course in Cyprus — a moment of accidentally inhaling water while watching animals, then having to manage inflation, depth, and direction at the same time.

Domain Knowledge

  • Real-world domain: open-water recreational diving in coastal and deep water.
  • Novice misconception: beginners often touch or approach any creature they see and assume the current is irrelevant.
  • Expert judgment: experienced divers read the current, plan a route that uses depth strategically, and keep one hand free to put a surface marker before surfacing.
  • Why this knowledge is playable: the entire game is one decision under oxygen pressure — when to go up, when to go down, when to go around, and when to leave a signal.

System Design

The system graph (provided by the student) maps four things: Win/Game Over conditions, Player Input bindings, Game Environment data, and the three level presets plus the data that is exposed on the in-game HUD.

  • Environment data: water current direction (Vector3), depth (surface / middle / deep), obstacle (reef) positions (Vector3), sea-creature direction and position (Vector3), oxygen amount (int), player HP (int), target position (Vector3), water current position and direction (Vector3).
  • Player-controlled data: turn, depth, forward/stop, put signal, catch (approach + W).
  • Calculated results: HP delta, distance to target, oxygen delta, time-on-surface / time-in-deep.
  • Feedback: weak current = slight view shake + bubble sound; strong current = heavy shake + bump + HP -50%; dangerous species = red edge + HP drain; surface with inflated BCD but no signal > 2 s = boat collision; inflate underwater = instant death (barotrauma); deep > 3 s = HP drain.
  • Challenge presets: Level 1 (no current, safe species, no obstacles) → Level 2 (weak current, dangerous species, scattered reefs) → Level 3 (strong current, attackers, many obstacles, low temperature, low starting oxygen).

Play the Game

Use W/S to move, A/D to turn, H/L to change depth, O to drop a surface signal, I to inflate BCD (at surface only!), R to restart the level.
