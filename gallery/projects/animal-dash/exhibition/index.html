Game Idea

Concept

Different animals have different characteristics and adapt to different environments. In Animal Dash, you race across randomly generated terrains — dodging obstacles with ← → keys, boosting speed with ↑ key, and switching to the right animal for each environment. Manage two survival resources: Stamina (energy, constantly draining) and HP (blood, reduced by obstacles). With 7 environments, 14 animals, and 3 levels of increasing difficulty, every run is different!

Player Goal

Survive and outrun the AI opponent by strategically switching animals at each terrain boundary. Dodge obstacles to preserve HP, collect food to restore stamina, and use the boost wisely.

Core Learning Shift

Novice misconception: Beginners just choose the fastest animal (e.g., Cheetah with speed 10) and hold boost all the time.
Expert insight: Consider multiple aspects — environment adaptation, stamina drain, HP toughness, and terrain fit. A Camel (speed 5, HP 60) beats a Cheetah in the desert because of perfect adaptation, high stamina, and better obstacle resistance. Adaptation is hidden — you learn it through experience!

Controls

🎮 ← → (or A/D) — Switch lanes to dodge obstacles and collect bonuses
🔥 ↑ (or W) hold — Boost speed ×1.5, but stamina drains ×2.2 faster!
🖱️ Click — Select animals and navigate menus
⌨️ Enter/Space — Progress through screens

Domain Knowledge: Animal Adaptation & Survival

Real-World Domain

Animals evolve characteristics suited to their native habitats. A dolphin is perfectly adapted to water but helpless on land. A mountain goat can traverse steep cliffs that a horse cannot. A penguin thrives in antarctic ice. This biological reality creates trade-offs: specialization means excellence in one environment but weakness in others. In the game, this extends to HP (blood/toughness) — a Bear is slow but nearly impossible to kill, while a Rabbit is quick but fragile.

Why This Becomes Playable

The trade-off between specialization and versatility is inherently game-like. Players must observe the terrain, judge which animal fits, and act by switching. The feedback is immediate — the animal speeds up or struggles visibly. Through repetition, players develop the expert skill of matching adaptation to environment. Adaptation is hidden — players discover which animals fit each terrain through experience, not by reading a number.

System Design

Environment Data

7 terrain types: grassland, forest, desert, ocean, mountain, antarctic, arctic. Each level generates random terrain segments — no two runs are the same! Level 1 announces upcoming terrain; Level 2 & 3 don't.

Player-Controlled Data

Lane position (← → keys) — dodge obstacles and collect bonuses.
Boost (↑ hold) — speed ×1.5, stamina drain ×2.2
Animal choice — switch at each terrain boundary.

System-Calculated Results

Effective Speed = baseSpeed × adaptation × staminaFactor × hpFactor × boost × acceleration
Stamina Drain = rate based on adaptation (+ boost multiplier)
HP Loss = obstacleHit × (3.0 - adaptation × 2.5)
Acceleration = ×(1 + time × 0.0008)

Feedback Translation

Speed bar — green/yellow/red
Stamina bar — green/yellow/red (depleting constantly)
HP bar — red (depleted by obstacles)
Boost flames 🔥 — visible behind animal when boosting
Collision flash — red screen on obstacle hit
Red border — flashing when HP low
Yellow border — flashing when stamina low
Ambient particles — snow/leaves/bubbles per terrain

Success & Failure

Success: Complete all terrain segments and cover more distance than the AI opponent. Survive by managing stamina (collect food), HP (dodge obstacles), and choosing well-adapted animals. The opponent is tough — winning by distance is the primary victory path.
Failure: Three ways to lose:
💀 Stamina = 0 — animal collapses from exhaustion
💀 HP = 0 — animal dies from too many obstacle hits
🏁 Opponent finishes first — you covered less distance

Game speed gradually increases over time within each level! Obstacles appear more frequently and sometimes spawn in pairs at higher difficulties. The opponent gets 1.5× stamina, 1.3× HP, slower drain, and stamina regeneration.

Core Player Learning Loop

1. Observe

See the terrain and its obstacles. Level 1 shows upcoming terrain; later levels don't. Watch your stamina and HP bars. Notice the ambient visual effects (snow, sand, leaves).

2. Judge

At terrain boundaries, which animal might fit? Adaptation is hidden — use your experience from previous runs. Consider Speed, Stamina, and HP.

3. Act

Switch to a promising animal, or keep your current one. Use ← → to dodge obstacles, ↑ to boost when needed. Collect food 🍖 and ❤️ hearts.

4. Read Feedback

Speed bar (green = thriving, red = struggling). Stamina depleting fast? HP dropping from hits? Opponent pulling ahead? All visible!

5. Adjust

If HP is low → dodge obstacles more carefully. If stamina is low → stop boosting, collect food. If speed is slow → switch animal next terrain. The opponent won't die — you need to outrun them by distance!
