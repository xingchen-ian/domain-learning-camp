Hidden Tides

A sailing survival game about judging what is truly safe.

The Big Idea

You captain a small sailboat across a dangerous sea, from the left shore to the finish line on the right.

The catch: The routes that look safest — wide open water with few stones — are exactly where sharks hide. Stone fields look dangerous, but they're actually your refuge from sharks (just don't crash into them at full speed).

This game teaches players to question what looks safe and to read warning signs before danger arrives.

Core Learning Shift

Before playing: Open water = safe. Stones = obstacles to avoid.

After playing: Open water = hidden danger. Stone fields = genuine safety zones.

The player learns that things that seem useless or threatening may actually be helpful, and that what appears safe can be the most dangerous place of all.

How to Play

  • WASD / Arrow Keys: Sail your boat in any direction
  • Space: Attack nearby sharks and pirates (costs energy)
  • R: Use a repair stone to heal your boat
  • P: Pause / Resume

Navigate around rocks, avoid or defeat sharks and pirates, watch for pirate flags on the horizon, listen for wind warnings, collect repair stones from crates (some are empty decoys!), and rest in pink river zones to recover health and energy.

Three Challenge Presets

  • Calm Passage: Fewer enemies, more time. Good for learning.
  • Shark Waters: More sharks in open water, moderate wind. Forces you near the rocks.
  • Storm Run: Frequent strong winds, many enemies, only one rest zone. The real test.
